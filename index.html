entry02: twentieth.

2026-03-16

Today is my birthday, and today I turned 20. I don't usually expect bad things to happen on any particular day, except two. Those two are my birthday and Christmas. I don't usually share this with anyone, since it comes off pretty sad when you think about it. It's strange to be sad on the two days which are supposed to be the happiest days of the year. However, almost every year the happiest days, for me, are never these two. This isn't to say that today was particularly a bad day. In fact, It was a day that I have been hoping to have for a while. Today I got up early and was really able to relax.

see more

Admittedly, I was supposed to go to Mexico today, as part of my spring break plans. I paid about $500 and was supposed to be on a plane at 8:00am this morning to leave for Puerto Vallarta. I got to the airport and tried checking in, but the Southwest worker told me that I didn't appear in the database of customers that were supposed to fly that day. I was then told that the only way I could get on the flight was to pay another $500 to secure a seat on the plane I was originally supposed to be on. I wasn't ready to pay that much, so instead I sat on hold with the travel agency which had officiated the purchase of my original flights. The process of getting a refund took about six hours. By the end of it, I was exhausted and slightly annoyed that my birthday had started off so poorly. This is exactly why I usually hate my birthday. I set high expectations for a great day, and generally it doesn't ever go perfectly. Something similar to this usually happens. Last year I was stuck in a big fight with my ex-girlfriend, and the year before I remember getting really sick. Nevertheless, I'm never really able to see all of the people who are important to me on that day, nor would I really want to. I usually want to rest. If it is a day that's supposed to be "all about me", usually the only things that I want to do are to sleep, eat, and enjoy doing the things I love. I imagine those things to be reading or writing.

Today, even though my plans fell through, I was able to spend the day resting in exactly the way I wanted to. I caught up on lost sleep, I read a book I've been meaning to read, and I was able to get started on writing a paper I've been meaning to write for school. These are all things that I seriously enjoyed. Yes, I didn't get to see my friends, or spend time with my family, but I got to do what I love. Those being the simple things that rapidly changing life doesn't usually let me enjoy.

I did get some other bad news today. I had been planning on interning at a law firm in Washington D.C. over the summer, and they had basically promised me a position once they had finalized the program. Today, they too, told me that they would be unable to give a secure position at the firm. This news came to me at the exact same time that I was pleading to get let on my flight. Admittedly, I was very tired in the morning, since I had to be up at 5 to pack and get ready to leave, so this collection of bad news just made me sad. I sat outside the Oakland airport, on a bench that smelled so fiercely of urine that it made my nostrils sting, and smoked a cigarette. The cigarette didn't really solve any of my problems, but it certainly did put them into perspective. Sure, I wouldn't be able to leave for Mexico that day, and I likely wouldn't be going to DC over the summer, but I still felt okay. I understood that I would be able to spend the day at home, which is one of the few places that I really love to be. Why would I stay sad about it? I've been busy with school, work, and friends, but today, I would really get to relax and enjoy my break at home. Regardless of the status of my internship, I knew I would keep looking. Similarly, I would keep looking for flights.

Thanks to my friends, I was able to find a round trip to Mexico that leaves tomorrow, for the same price that I had paid before. I was also able to get back to some messages on Facebook marketplace that I had been putting off, and now it's looking likely that I'll sell my truck. Things, in a sense, are looking up. This is usually how it goes, and this type of clarity really only comes on the days that you expect to be great. They give you hope for the next year. I keep hope for the whole year that Christmas or my birthday will be amazing, and although they never really live up to the expectations I set, they offer something completely different. They offer me a sense of hope, that as long as I don't let the unfortunate parts of life keep me down, then perhaps I'll get to feel what it's like to have a great birthday.

I'm excited for the next year of my life. Things change so rapidly now that I never really know what to expect. Yes, that brings with it uncertainty, which is certainly uncomfortable (haha), but it is in this uncertainty that I hope to learn new things about myself, as I always do. It's a gift to live, and although sometimes I live in fear, I still know the former to be true. I hope that my twentieth year will be a great one, in the truest sense of the word, great in good or in bad, both offering something new and exciting that I haven't felt before. Hopefully my twenty-first will be as great as my twentieth.

P.S. — I got lots of amazing birthday wishes today. It always made me feel better when I could read them. I'll write them all now, so that I can hopefully remember them in the future. Thank you to: Isaac, Eliot, Jonah, Jo, Asher, James, Anne, Trey, Gio, Stavos, Nik, Rylan, Baker, Tobey, Becca, Austin, Kaleb, Kyle, Maggie, Amir, Gabe, Leila, Sia, Nico, Teddy, Ben and Sadie. Thanks to everyone who sang happy birthday to me on the phone. Special thanks to Analize, a dear friend of mine who likely forgot my birthday, but I know she would have said something had she known (I'm usually very private about my birthday). A similarly special thanks to Kai, Jonah, Noah, and Soren who all wished me a happy birthday this past weekend when I was with them. But the most thanks is to my family: Tosha, Mom, and Dad. Thank you all for being a part of my life, and words can't really describe how much it means to me that you all give any part of yourself to be in my life. Eternally grateful for all of you. It would take 10 more pages to write gratitudes worth any of your character in this letter, but ultimately just: Thank you.

entry01: Carpe Diem!

2026-03-09

Since coming to college, life has felt particularly heavy to me. I'll be speaking candidly, hoping not to recount the events of my past, as beautiful and as painful as some of them have been, but instead trying to recount some of the lessons I have learned and how I hope to approach life in the future.

see more

Lately I've felt an intensity of emotions surrounding shame. I'll lay in bed often, recounting the mistakes that I've made and the people that I have hurt. However, most of this shame comes from a selfish place, as it slices apart my ego and makes my heart bleed. The perceptions of myself that I used to carry have slowly evaporated over the past couple of years and have left me feeling hollow. I used to pride myself on my unwavering generosity and understanding for others, and although I still hold these things to be true about myself, I have also noticed that my personal feelings of hopelessness and loneliness now cloud my judgement in social interactions to a degree that leads to me act cruelly or at least without courtesy for others. This is selfish in the sense that I mourn more the loss of these favorable traits than I do the hurt which I have catalyzed. For this I forgive myself. This, for me, is particularly difficult. However, it's important to understand for any person, but especially myself, that forgiving your imperfect past is the first step in making meaningful amends to a world which you may have stained. Nobody can totally understand the depth of colors which tint the glasses which God has placed upon my eyes, and for the inverse: I can hear people's words, but I can never judge their hearts.

Throughout the struggle of moving through a world in which I occasionally feel misplaced, I have also found a clarity which I only see rarely. In this clarity, it's undoubtable that the first place in which my mind and my heart should go – is love. However, it's difficult to love an imperfect world when it seems to shame you for your own imperfections. The contradiction in this though, is that the only true source of shame is the self. Yes, you may hear murmurs of others speaking badly about you, or criticizing your behavior, but this is just noise. Unless it comes to you from a place of love and understanding, as it would from a dear friend, these words hold no weight. It's impossible to predict why someone may have said something cruel about you without trying to first understand your struggle, but to judge them for this behavior would be no different than to embody the trait that I seem to disdain. Adjacently, this is a trait that I can confidently say that I hate, however it is not the people who embody it who I hate, as I too have given into the temptations of judgement. The struggle through the game of life is something that all people can understand and empathize with, even if they can't perfectly understand the depth of emotions which might flow through your mind at any given moment, in this I must have faith.

Speaking less about others, and more about myself now, I'll recount how life has felt lately. I look in the mirror and see a bright sun of limitless potential, in a way that seems irrational, but I also notice the things which I tell myself to hate. I see a judgemental, unhealthy, anxious, and pathetic person with lost potential. This has framed my thinking for the greater part of this semester. I worry that I have no real friends, or that those around me despise me and say cruelties behind my back. I see the consequences of my actions, and although to some they are humorous, they also hurt others. This I have had to wear with a feign confidence which comes from a carelessness about my own life or perception, akin to giving into the absurd and simply living in whichever way I desire at any moment. This though, creates the hollowness that I am left with – when alone. Since living with this debate in my head, of whether I am simply discovering myself, or acting foolishly, I have discovered that it is uniquely both. Yes, I have acted like a fool, dumber than I know myself to be, prioritizing any amount of social validation which I could possibly earn, instead of being true to myself. However, this mistake has led me to realize that it was a necessary step to discover more about who I truly am, and it brings me closer to revealing what ideas should be firmly etched into my soul. I compare it to Michelangelo carving David (although I am certainly not as talented of an artist as Michaelangelo), as I consider myself the sculptor of my soul. Michaelangelo claimed that he first saw David in the marble, and slowly revealed him through careful strikes of his chisel. I see a version of myself that loves myself, and similarly loves the world. But on this path, I must make strikes which may chip away at more than is necessary, or leave behind parts that should not remain. But eventually, I will be left with the vision which I see. It's undoubtable that we all make mistakes in our lives, some bigger and more consequential than others, but if we never forgive others how can we expect to ever forgive ourselves? If we hold this to be true, then it must also be true that the thing which we should hold closest to our hearts is love. Love for others, their imperfections, their scorn, their triumphs, their competition and most importantly, for their comradery. For if we do not love all the parts of life which carefully challenge us, then we will never be able to love ourselves. Your closest friend is also your most dangerous enemy, able to break your heart in ways which you cannot even imagine, but this does not mean to avoid closeness, but instead it is the beautiful gamble of life.

The way to win the game is to realize that there is no game at all. Life is not a series of events neatly ordered to give meaning to your existence, as you tirelessly work to reach the next level of "success", whether it be getting into your dream college, landing your dream job, or being the most loved on your college campus (all things I have certainly desired). The way to "win", so to speak, is to recognize that life is no different at any individual stage of life as it was in the past. It has always just been, and that being said, what can possibly be more eternal and deep than approaching your own life from a position of purity and candor. Be honest about your mistakes, apologize, but don't expect any recognition. Not that it would matter, as the sweetest forgiveness comes only from the self and becoming who you wish you always were. Life changes when you decide it is time, not when all of the pieces finally fall neatly into place.

To return to the name of the entry, it is important that you carry these facts of life in your darkest moments. In my case, when it feels that I have to rebuild my life after having no real friends, struggle with my own sense of identity after committing myself in my entirety to something fleeting, or when you have changed so much that you must humble yourself enough to realize that being hurt and making mistakes is necessary to accomplish the self-actualization which you desire. Sometimes these thoughts will fill me with hope and love, and other times it will take everything in my power to not give in to the evil which stains my heart. At times like these, it is important to speak kindly to yourself and take care of yourself, remembering "What punishments of God are not gifts?". Your potential is limitless, defined by none other than yourself, and allows you to live the way in which you desire. Continuing, the solutions to the natural questions which arise, following these affirmations, of , "How do I want to live?" and "What do I desire?" are uniquely to be at peace and to love endlessly. Accept others as they are, and accept yourself as you are. Keep your heart in the right place and may you never feel shame again. Seize the day! It's a gift to be molded by the pressures of life.

P.S. If you are not me, and you happen to be reading this, thank you. I am writing this as a memoir to myself, and as a public record of my thoughts, as to hopefully never betray these thoughts.
