entry01: Carpe Diem!

2026-03-09

Since coming to college, life has felt particularly heavy to me. I'll be speaking candidly, hoping not to recount the events of my past, as beautiful and as painful as some of them have been, but instead trying to recount some of the lessons I have learned and how I hope to approach life in the future.

see more

Lately I've felt an intensity of emotions surrounding shame. I'll lay in bed often, recounting the mistakes that I've made and the people that I have hurt. However, most of this shame comes from a selfish place, as it slices apart my ego and makes my heart bleed. The perceptions of myself that carry have slowly evaporated over the past couple of years and have left me feeling hollow. I used to pride myself on my unwavering generosity and understanding for others, and although I still hold these things to be true about myself, I have also noticed that my personal feelings of hopelessness and loneliness now cloud my judgement in social interactions to a degree that leads to me act cruelly or at least without courtesy for others. This is selfish in the sense that I mourn more the loss of these favorable traits than I do the hurt which I have catalyzed. For this I forgive myself. This, for me, is particularly difficult. However, it's important to understand for any person, but especially myself, that forgiving your imperfect past is the first step in making meaningful amends to a world which you may have stained. Nobody can totally understand the depth of colors which tint the glasses that God has placed upon my eyes, and in the inverse: I can hear people's words, but I can never feel their hearts.

Throughout the struggle of moving through a world in which I occasionally feel misplaced, I have also found a clarity which I only see rarely. In this clarity, it's undoubtable that the first place in which my mind and my heart should go – is love. However, it's difficult to love an imperfect world when it seems to shame you for your own imperfections. The contradiction in this is though, that the only true source of shame comes from the self. Yes, you may hear murmurs of others speaking badly about you, or criticizing your behavior, but this is just noise. Unless it comes to you from a place of love and understanding, as it would from a dear friend, these words hold no weight. It's impossible to predict why someone may have said something cruel about you without trying to first understand your struggle, but to judge them for this behavior would be no different than to embody the trait that I seem to disdain. Adjacently, this is a trait that I can confidently say that I hate, however it is not the people who embody it who I hate, as I too have given into the temptations of judgement. The struggle through the game of life is something that all people can understand and empathize with, even if they can't perfectly understand the depth of emotions which might flow through your mind at any given moment, in this I must have faith.

Speaking less about others, and more about myself now, I'll recount how life has felt lately. I look in the mirror and see a bright sun of limitless potential, in a way that seems insane, but I also notice the things which I tell myself to hate. I see a judgemental, unhealthy, anxious, and pathetic person with lost potential. This has framed my thinking for the greater part of this semester. I worry that I have no real friends, or that those around me despise me and say cruelties behind my back. I see the consequences of my actions, and although to some they are humorous, they also hurt others. This I have had to wear with a feign confidence which comes from a carelessness about my own life or perception, akin to giving into the absurd and simply living in whichever way I desire at any moment. This though, creates the hollowness that I am left with – when alone. Since living with this debate in my head, of whether I am simply discovering myself, or acting foolishly, I have discovered that it is uniquely both. Yes, I have acted like a fool, dumber than I know myself to be, prioritizing any amount of social validation which I could possibly earn, instead of being true to myself. However, this mistake has led me to realize that it was a necessary step to discover more about who I truly am, and it brings me closer to revealing what ideas should be firmly etched into my soul. I compare it to Michelangelo carving David (although I am certainly not as talented of an artist as Michaelangelo), as I consider myself the sculptor of my soul. Michaelangelo claimed that he first saw David in the marble, and slowly revealed him through careful strikes of his chisel. I see a version of myself that loves myself, and similarly loves the world. But on this path, I must make strikes which may chip away at more than is necessary, or leave behind parts that should not remain. But eventually, I will be left with the vision which I see. It's undoubtable that we all make mistakes in our lives, some bigger and more consequential than others, but if we never forgive others how can we expect to ever forgive ourselves? If we hold this to be true, then it must also be true that the thing which we should hold closest to our hearts is love. Love for others, love for their imperfections, love for their scorn, love for their triumphs, love for their competition and most importantly, love for their comradery. For if we do not love all the parts of life which carefully challenge us, then we will never be able to love ourselves. Your closest friend is also your most capable enemy, able to break your heart in ways which you cannot even imagine, but this does not mean to avoid closeness, but instead it is the beautiful gamble of life.

The way to win the game is to realize that there is no game at all. Life is not a series of events neatly ordered to give meaning to your existence, as you tirelessly work to reach the next level of "success", whether it be getting into your dream college, landing your dream job, or being the most loved on your college campus (these are all things I have certainly desired). The way to "win", so to speak, is to recognize that life is no different at any individual stage of life as it was in the past. It has always just been, and that being said, what can possibly be more eternal and deep than approaching your own life from a position of purity and candor. Be honest about your mistakes, apologize, but don't expect any recognition for this behavior. Not that it would matter, the sweetest forgiveness comes only from forgiving yourself and being who you wish you always were. Life changes when you decide it is time, not when all of the pieces finally fall neatly into place.

To return to the name of the entry, it is important that you carry these facts of life in your darkest moments. In my case, when it feels that I have to rebuild my life after having no real friends, struggle with my own sense of identity after committing myself in my entirety to something fleeting, or when you have changed so much that you must humble yourself enough to realize that being hurt and making mistakes is necessary to accomplish the self-actualization which you desire. Sometimes these thoughts will fill me with hope and love, and other times it will take everything in my power to not give in to the evil which stains my heart. At times like these, it is important to speak kindly to yourself and take care of yourself, remembering "What punishments of God are not gifts?". Your potential is limitless, defined by none other than yourself, and allows you to live the way in which you desire. Continuing, the solutions to the natural questions which arise, following these affirmations, of , "How do I want to live?" and "What do I desire?" are uniquely to be at peace and to love endlessly. Accept others as they are, and accept yourself as you are. Keep your heart in the right place and may you never feel shame again. Seize the day! It's a gift to be molded by the pressures of life.

P.S. If you are not me, and you happen to be reading this, thank you. I am writing this as a memoir to myself, and as a public record of my thoughts, as to hopefully never betray these thoughts.
